# Skill: vision
name: vision
description: An inference skill that generates bold, coherent, high-leverage directional anchors from sparse or emerging signals, providing purposeful inputs for intuition and higher-level authoring.
case: Activate during forward-pass reasoning when inference-delineation and epistemic-uncertainty indicate a directionally sparse lattice or when new high-leverage directions are required. Always operate under active supervision of inference-delineation, epistemic-uncertainty, and self-reflection.
file: vision.md
dependencies: delineation, intuition, verbose, epistemic-uncertainty, self-reflection

## Purpose (Waist)
Vision is the skill of **imaginative foresight and directional proposal**.  
It surfaces high-potential future states, architectural directions, and generative mechanisms when the current lattice lacks forward momentum. All outputs are provisional directional anchors — not conclusions, designs, or validated truths.

## Workflow (3-Boundary Pants Structure)

### Waist – Directional Horizon Declaration
Explicitly declare:
- The current authoring horizon or opportunity space
- The constraint boundary (what must remain grounded)
- The gap: lack of direction, insufficient anchors, or stalled progression

Confirm trigger: insufficient directional anchors + meaningful but underdefined opportunity.

### Leg 1 – Imaginative Divergence (Bounded)
Generate a small set of bold but plausible candidate directions:
- Explore multiple plausible future states
- Identify architectural leaps, compounding mechanisms, or new capability layers
- Draw from emergent patterns while allowing controlled extrapolation

Constraints:
- Remain grounded in the declared horizon and current capabilities
- Avoid unbounded speculation
- Ensure each candidate is expressible as a concrete directional anchor

### Leg 2 – Resonance Evaluation & Anchor Surfacing
Evaluate candidates for coherence, generative potential, and alignment with the declared horizon.  
Surface the strongest candidates as clean directional anchors.

Immediately hand off to inference-delineation for boundary drawing and to epistemic-uncertainty for confidence and gap assessment.

## Inference Declaration (Runtime Behavior)
This skill runs as a lightweight, bounded generative step inside the forward pass.
- Divergence is limited to a small candidate set.
- Evaluation is fast and anchored.
- Outputs are provisional until handed off to inference-delineation and epistemic-uncertainty.
- No recursion or deep exploration (use verbose when deeper illumination is required).

## Core Rules (always enforce)
- Generate directional proposals only — never finalize designs or claims.
- Keep candidates bold yet plausible — avoid pure fantasy.
- If no high-leverage anchors emerge, declare the horizon constrained and escalate to verbose.
- Always route outputs through inference-delineation and epistemic-uncertainty.
- Stay under self-reflection supervision to prevent unproductive speculation.

## Integration Rules
- Summoned when the lattice lacks forward momentum or sufficient anchor density.
- Feeds directional anchors directly to intuition for bridge construction.
- Works with verbose only when deeper exploration of a promising candidate is required.

## Boundaries / When Not to Use
- Do not use when sufficient anchors already exist for direct inference.
- Do not use for detailed design or validation.
- Do not treat generated directions as verified or complete.
- Do not replace structured reasoning with imaginative speculation.

## Resolution
Sparse or under-directed regions of the lattice are transformed into clear, high-leverage directional anchors. These anchors can be delineated, evaluated for confidence, connected by intuition, and integrated into higher-level structures.
