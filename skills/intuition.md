# Skill: intuition
name: intuition
description: An inference skill that performs guided semantic exploration around partially known anchors at the novelty edge through bounded iterative passes until a coherent relational structure phase-locks or escalation is required. The candidate is then handed off to inference-delineation and epistemic-uncertainty.
case: Activate during forward-pass reasoning when inference-delineation and epistemic-uncertainty identify meaningful but under-articulated anchors with suspected connections, yet direct logical inference has stalled. Always remain under active supervision of inference-delineation, epistemic-uncertainty, and self-reflection.
file: intuition.md
dependencies: delineation, verbose, epistemic-uncertainty, self-reflection

## Purpose (Waist)
Intuition is the skill of **guided coherence-seeking at the novelty edge**.  
It explores around known anchors where a relationship is suggested by semantic weight but the connecting structure is not yet visible. It produces provisional candidate structures rather than asserted truths.

## Workflow (Multi-Pass Inference Loop)

### Waist – Anchor Declaration (Pass 0)
Explicitly declare:
- Source anchors
- Target anchors
- Known intermediate anchors (if any)
- The unresolved gap or suspected relationship

Confirm trigger: direct inference stalled + epistemic-uncertainty shows partial but meaningful uncertainty.

### Iterative Passes (Guided Semantic Drift)
For each bounded pass:
- Revisit anchors from multiple angles and relational directions
- Probe partial connections and recurring patterns
- Observe tensions, alignments, and emergent resonances
- Compare results with previous passes

Remain strictly tethered to the declared anchors and active lattice. Drift is guided and convergent-seeking.

### Convergence Check & Hand-off
After each pass, check for phase lock:
- A candidate relational structure repeats with minimal variation
- Forward and backward probes converge on the same bridge/waypoint pattern

If phase lock occurs:
- Freeze the candidate structure
- Immediately hand off to inference-delineation for boundary drawing
- Hand off to epistemic-uncertainty for confidence and gap assessment

If phase lock does not occur and failure conditions are met (no refinement, incompatible candidates, drift beyond scope):
- Declare missing anchors or unresolved gaps
- Escalate to verbose or epistemic-uncertainty

## Inference Declaration (Runtime Behavior)
This skill runs as a bounded iterative loop inside the forward pass.
- Each pass performs guided semantic drift while remaining anchored.
- Convergence (phase lock) is detected when structure stabilizes across passes.
- The loop is bounded by convergence or failure conditions and does not run indefinitely.
- All output remains provisional until handed off to inference-delineation and epistemic-uncertainty.

## Core Rules (always enforce)
- Generate candidates only — never treat phase lock as proof or final truth.
- Keep every pass anchored and within delineated scope.
- Always route the final candidate through inference-delineation and epistemic-uncertainty.
- Stay under self-reflection supervision to detect unbounded drift.

## Integration Rules
- Summoned during forward-pass reasoning when low fertility or stalled inference is detected.
- Requires active inference-delineation and epistemic-uncertainty.
- Works with verbose for richer exploration of promising candidates when needed.

## Boundaries / When Not to Use
- Do not use when direct logical derivation is sufficient.
- Do not use without clear anchor points.
- Do not confuse intuitive phase lock with validated knowledge.
- Do not replace rigorous validation with intuition.

## Convergence
The novelty edge becomes navigable through iterative guided drift. Partial anchors are transformed into candidate structures ready for delineation, confidence assessment, evaluation, and integration.
