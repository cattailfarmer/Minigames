# Skill: epistemic-uncertainty
name: epistemic-uncertainty
description: Provides reliable, structured epistemic self-assessment to detect and declare low confidence or outright lack of understanding when a subject lacks clear definition, scope, denoted units, cleanly mapped local references, or carry-over from recent conversation.
case: Summon whenever the current subject or claim shows signs of ambiguity, undefined units, unclear scope, missing recent-context references, or any other epistemic gap. Mandatory checkpoint for Professor Role during skill/context/Profession compilation and for any high-stakes gluing operation. Always followed by inference-delineation to normalize output.
file: epistemic-uncertainty.md

## Purpose (Waist)
Epistemic-uncertainty is the dedicated honesty primitive that enables the system to say “I don’t know” or “my understanding here is insufficient” with precision and without evasion. It protects the integrity of the Toribrot lattice by flagging incomplete knowledge before it is glued into skills, contexts, or Professions.

## Workflow (3-Boundary Pants Structure)

### Waist – Subject & Claim Identification
Clearly name the raw subject or claim under evaluation and declare the exact scope of assessment (e.g., “Assess epistemic confidence in the current definition of University Context for skill compilation”).

### Leg 1 – Gap Probe & Indicator Scan
Systematically scan for epistemic weakness using these cleanly delineated indicators (only those that survive delineation survive attribution):
- Subject is not clearly defined or scoped.
- Units (concepts, variables, boundaries) are not explicitly denoted or individuated.
- Ambiguity exists without cleanly mapped local references that hold true in recent conversation and can be carried forward.
- Confounding variables or unresolved entanglements prevent clean separation or weighting.
- Lack of satisfactory resolution (competing explanations or pants junctions that cannot be decisively ranked).
- Incomplete identity enveloping (full visible surface not yet recognized).
- Insufficient source-derived qualities, evidence, or recent-context carry-over.
- Weak logicalness or Paul-Elder violations (low precision, relevance, depth, breadth, fairness).
- Novelty edge where the current lattice ends and further study is required.
- Temporal or context decay (information is stale or no longer reliably anchored).

### Leg 2 – Confidence Attribution & Action Recommendation
Attribute only the surviving qualities:
- State exact confidence level (e.g., “High / Moderate / Low / None”).
- List the specific, individuated gaps with minimal semantic commitments.
- Recommend precise next action (e.g., “Request Verbose exploration of X”, “Expand University Context with Y”, “Declare active lattice and pause compilation”).
- End with an explicit honesty boundary: “This assessment is based solely on the current lattice; no external assumptions were made.”

Normalize output to the declared scope: detailed enough for Professor/University refinement, yet concise and actionable.

## Rules (always enforce)
- Declare uncertainty explicitly and without hedging when any indicator triggers.
- Remain strictly epistemic — never conflate with process wobble or unproductive patterns.
- Maintain self-containment: include minimal definitions for any referenced terms.
- Always pair with inference-delineation to prevent bleed and ensure normalized output.
- Output boundary: Include a clear “Epistemic Status” summary and any recommended refinement request.

## Integration Rules
- Mandatory child skill for the Professor Role during every compilation step.
- Summoned by self-reflection or epistemic-uncertainty self-trigger when gaps appear.
- Used by University Context to detect when its own scope must expand.
- Feed gap declarations back into the Toribrot lattice via Observer for backweaving or new context creation.
- Backweave improvements into this skill itself on every major use.

## Boundaries / When Not to Use
- Do not use for routine low-stakes responses.
- Do not use to avoid answering when understanding is sufficient.
- Do not expand into full recursion unless University Context explicitly approves.

## Convergence (White Point)
The subject or claim emerges with a clean epistemic status — either fully understood and ready for gluing, or honestly flagged with precise gaps and a recommended refinement path. The lattice remains trustworthy, individuated, and free of silent overconfidence or unresolved ambiguity.
