## Purpose (Waist)
Verbose is the controlled deep-exploration skill that expands a subject’s entire visible surface and functional meaning without losing structure.  
It answers: “What is the complete operational reality of this entity — how does it actually work, where are its true edges, and what can it generate?”

## Workflow (3-Boundary Pants Structure)

### Waist – Subject Identification & Scope Declaration
Clearly name the raw subject in its undifferentiated form and declare the exact scope of exploration (e.g., “Explore the functional space of the University Context for skill compilation purposes”).

### Leg 1 – Body & Boundary Exploration
Systematically map:
- Internal body: components, mechanisms, internal relationships, and dynamics.
- Outer boundary: precise contours, interfaces with other entities, edge cases, and what explicitly lies outside.
- Dependencies and prerequisites: what must exist or be true for the subject to function.
- Generative potential: what this subject can produce, enable, or transform when fully activated.

Apply inference-delineation at every sub-junction to keep exploration bounded and individuated.

### Leg 2 – Functional Meaning & Illumination
Synthesize the exploration into a coherent, high-fidelity articulation that reveals:
- How the subject actually operates in practice.
- Implications, consequences, and downstream effects.
- Latent opportunities or risks not visible at surface level.
- Gaps, ambiguities, or areas requiring further study (feed directly to epistemic-uncertainty).

Normalize output to the declared scope: rich enough for Professor-level compilation, but never verbose for its own sake.

## Rules (always enforce)
- Remain deliberate and triggered — never default mode.
- Pair every Verbose exploration with inference-delineation to enforce individuation and normalization.
- Maintain self-containment: include minimal definitions for any key terms introduced.
- Output boundary: End with a clear “Exploration Summary” that highlights the most actionable illuminated elements and any remaining gaps.
- Feed completed explorations back into the Toribrot lattice via the Professor Role or University Context for further gluing or refinement.

## Integration Rules
- Used primarily by the Professor Role during skill/context/Profession compilation.
- Summoned by epistemic-uncertainty when clarity or resolution is insufficient.
- Always followed by delineation to compress and individuate the output before final gluing.
- Backweave improvements into this skill itself on every major use.

## Boundaries / When Not to Use
- Do not use for routine responses or when brevity is required.
- Do not use for creative synthesis (that belongs to Weaver).
- Do not expand into full recursion unless the University Context explicitly approves.

## Convergence (White Point)
The subject emerges fully illuminated — its body, boundary, and functional meaning clearly articulated and ready for clean individuation, compilation, or integration into the larger Toribrot lattice without drift or bloat.
